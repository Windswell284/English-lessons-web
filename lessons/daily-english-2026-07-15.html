After Record Heat, Could the Atlantic Make Britain’s Weather Even More Extreme?

Today’s Wednesday science and climate lesson looks at ocean circulation, rising temperatures, and how changes in the Atlantic could reshape weather far beyond the sea.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: After record heat, could the Atlantic make Britain's weather even more extreme?

Source: BBC News

Published: July 14, 2026

Original article: BBC News article link

Article Summary

BBC News reports that scientists are studying whether the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation, known as AMOC, is weakening as the planet warms. The system carries warm water north and colder water south, helping shape storms, rainfall, winds, and temperatures around the North Atlantic and north-west Europe.

The article explains that a weaker AMOC would not simply cancel global warming, but it could make the UK’s weather more unstable, including colder winters, changed rainfall, and more volatile storm patterns. Scientists disagree about how quickly the system could shift, yet many say cutting emissions would reduce pressure on a climate system that societies cannot afford to push too far.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導，科學家正在研究大西洋經向翻轉環流（AMOC）是否會隨著地球暖化而減弱。這個系統把溫暖海水帶往北方、把較冷海水帶往南方，並影響北大西洋與西北歐洲的風暴、降雨、風向和氣溫。

文章指出，AMOC 減弱並不代表全球暖化會消失，但可能讓英國天氣更不穩定，包括較冷的冬季、降雨變化和更易波動的風暴型態。科學家對這個系統可能多快改變仍有不同看法，但許多人認為，減少排放能降低一個人類社會承受不起的氣候系統壓力。

5 Daily Words

volatile

likely to change suddenly and strongly; unstable or hard to predict.

In article: “As temperatures rise, scientists are investigating whether a changing Atlantic could bring more volatile weather to the UK.”

易變的、不穩定的；可能突然大幅改變、難以預測的。

circulation

the movement of something through a system, especially air, water, or blood moving around in a pattern.

In article: “It is called the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation, or AMOC - a vast, north-south system of currents that carries warm surface water towards the Arctic and returns colder water thousands of miles south through the deep ocean.”

循環、流動；指空氣、水或血液等在系統中依某種模式移動。

monsoon

a seasonal wind and rain pattern, especially one that brings heavy rain to a region for part of the year.

In article: “A major shift could tug at the West African monsoon, tropical rainfall belts and rainfall over South America.”

季風；特定季節出現的風與降雨型態，常帶來大量降雨。

emissions

gases, heat, or other substances released into the air, especially greenhouse gases from human activity.

In article: “Cutting emissions does not remove every uncertainty. It does not guarantee the Atlantic will behave as scientists hope.”

排放物、排放量；特別指人類活動釋放到空氣中的溫室氣體等物質。

paleoclimate

the climate of an earlier period in Earth’s history, studied through evidence such as ice, rocks, shells, or sediment.

In article: “The lesson, he says, was that ‘the paleoclimate evidence actually doesn't suggest that climate change would happen smoothly, but rather in some jumps.’”

古氣候；透過冰層、岩石、貝殼或沉積物等證據研究的地球過去氣候。
