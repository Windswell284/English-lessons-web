Heat Pump Growth Stalls as UK Climate Advisers Warn on Home Heating

Today’s science and climate lesson uses a BBC News report about heat pumps, emissions targets, and the difficulty of making cleaner home heating affordable.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: Heat pump growth stalls as government support cut, warns climate watchdog

Source: BBC News

Published: June 23, 2026

Original article: BBC article link

Article Summary

BBC News reports that UK heat pump sales growth has slowed after the government cut a grant programme that helped lower-income households install the technology. The country’s climate advisers say overall carbon emissions are still falling, but slow progress in home heating could put future climate targets at risk.

The article explains that most UK homes still use gas boilers, while heat pumps run on electricity that can come from renewable sources. Advisers and industry figures argue that cleaner heating needs to become more affordable, especially because electricity bills remain high and installation costs can be difficult for households.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導，英國政府削減一項協助低收入家庭安裝熱泵的補助計畫後，熱泵銷售成長明顯放緩。英國氣候顧問表示，整體碳排放仍在下降，但家用供暖轉型進展緩慢，可能讓未來氣候目標面臨風險。

文章指出，英國多數家庭仍使用燃氣鍋爐，而熱泵使用電力運作，電力可以來自太陽能、風能等再生能源。顧問與業界人士認為，若要推動更乾淨的供暖方式，必須讓安裝與使用成本變得更可負擔。

5 Daily Words

growth

an increase in size, number, sales, or activity over time.

In article: “Heat pump growth stalls as government support cut, warns climate watchdog.”

成長、增加；數量、銷售或活動隨時間上升。

Usage note: In business and policy news, growth stalls means progress slows down or stops.

emissions

gases or substances released into the air, especially carbon dioxide from energy use, transport, or industry.

In article: “Overall, carbon emissions continued to fall, the advisors said, putting the UK in a ‘leading group of countries’- but slow progress in home heating risks future targets being missed.”

排放（物）；特別指能源、交通或工業活動釋放到空氣中的二氧化碳等氣體。

transition

a change from one system, condition, or way of doing things to another, usually over time.

In article: “‘This transition in our homes is no different to the ones we’ve had before. Fresh running water in homes, indoor sanitation, central heating, heat pumps.’”

轉型、過渡；從一種制度、狀態或做法逐步轉變到另一種。

Usage note: Climate news often uses transition for the move from fossil fuels to cleaner energy.

volatile

likely to change suddenly and unpredictably, especially prices, markets, or political situations.

In article: “‘We can see in the numbers what people want - cheap cars and cars that will save them money, particularly as fossil fuels are volatile,’ she said.”

波動大、不穩定的；特別指價格、市場或政治情勢可能突然且難以預測地改變。

prohibitively

so much or so great that it prevents people from doing or buying something.

In article: “However for most, heat pumps are prohibitively expensive to install.”

高到令人卻步地、阻礙性地；程度大到讓人無法負擔或不敢採取行動。

Usage note: This is today’s stretch word. It often appears before expensive, costly, or difficult.
