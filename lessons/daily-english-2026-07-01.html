Dangerous Heatwave to Hit US Ahead of Holiday Weekend

Today’s Wednesday science-and-climate lesson looks at extreme heat, public warnings, humidity, and the formal weather language used when dangerous conditions spread across a large region.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: Dangerous heatwave to hit US ahead of holiday weekend

Source: BBC News

Published: June 30, 2026

Original article: BBC News article link

Article Summary

BBC News reports that a prolonged heatwave is expected to intensify across a large part of the United States before the Fourth of July holiday weekend. Forecasters warned of scorching daytime heat, high humidity, and stifling overnight temperatures.

The article says about 120 million people were under an extreme heat warning as of Tuesday. Officials urged residents to limit time outdoors, stay hydrated, and use air conditioning or cooling centres, while parts of Canada also faced high temperatures and heat warnings.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導，美國大範圍地區在七月四日假期週末前將遭遇持續且危險的熱浪，而且熱浪預計會增強。氣象預報員警告，白天將出現酷熱、高濕度，夜間也會悶熱難耐。

文章指出，截至週二，全美約有一億兩千萬人處於極端高溫警告之下。官員呼籲居民減少戶外活動、保持水分，並使用冷氣或前往避暑中心；加拿大部分地區也面臨高溫與熱浪警報。

5 Daily Words

heatwave

a period of unusually hot weather that lasts for several days and may create health or safety risks.

In article: “A prolonged and dangerous heatwave will intensify across a swathe of the US this week...”

熱浪；持續數日、異常炎熱並可能造成健康或安全風險的天氣現象。

intensify

to become stronger, more serious, or more extreme; often used for weather, conflict, pressure, or debate.

In article: “A prolonged and dangerous heatwave will intensify across a swathe of the US this week...”

加劇、增強；變得更強烈、更嚴重或更極端，常用於天氣、衝突、壓力或爭論。

swathe

a large strip, area, or section of land, people, or activity.

In article: “A prolonged and dangerous heatwave will intensify across a swathe of the US this week...”

大片地區、一長片範圍；指土地、人群或活動中相當大的一部分。

stifling

so hot, humid, or airless that it feels difficult to breathe or be comfortable.

In article: “Ben Rich looks at the heatwave developing across the eastern United States bringing stifling heat and humidity.”

悶熱難耐的、令人窒息般的；形容高溫、高濕或空氣不流通到讓人不舒服。

unprecedented

never known, seen, or done before; used for events or conditions that are beyond normal historical experience.

In article: “It comes after Europe's unprecedented early summer heatwave scorched several countries over recent days...”

前所未有的、史無前例的；用來形容超出過往經驗、從未見過或發生過的事件或狀況。

Usage note: This is today’s stretch word. It is common in climate, legal, policy, and economic reporting when a situation has no clear recent comparison.
