Midnight Social Media Curfew Proposed for UK Teens Aged 16 and 17

Today’s Thursday technology lesson looks at online safety, teen screen time, age checks, and how governments try to regulate platform design.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: Midnight social media curfew proposed for UK teens aged 16 and 17

Source: BBC News

Published: July 14, 2026

Original article: BBC News article link

Article Summary

BBC News reports that the UK government has proposed a midnight-to-6 a.m. default social media curfew for 16- and 17-year-olds. Apps such as Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube would be unavailable by default during those hours, while features like auto-play and infinite scroll would also be disabled unless users change the settings.

The plan is meant to improve teenagers’ sleep, focus, and family life, but campaigners and experts are divided. Some say the measures do not go far enough because teens can switch them off; others worry a curfew could stop vulnerable young people from reaching support at night. The proposal adds to a wider debate about age verification, platform responsibility, and children’s online safety.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導，英國政府提議對 16 與 17 歲青少年設定社群媒體「午夜到早上六點」的預設宵禁。Instagram、TikTok、YouTube 等應用程式在這段時間會預設無法使用，而自動播放、無限捲動等功能也會預設停用，除非使用者自行更改設定。

政府表示，這項計畫是為了改善青少年的睡眠、專注力與家庭生活，但倡議人士與專家看法不一。有些人認為措施不夠強，因為青少年可以關閉限制；也有人擔心，宵禁可能讓脆弱的孩子在夜間無法取得支持。這項提案也反映出關於年齡驗證、平台責任與兒童網路安全的更大爭論。
