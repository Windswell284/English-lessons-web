Could Legal Personhood Help Save Lough Neagh?

Today’s Sunday lesson uses a lighter environment-and-law feature about whether giving nature legal rights could help restore a polluted lake in Northern Ireland.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: Could legal 'personhood' help save Lough Neagh?

Source: BBC News

Published: June 27, 2026

Original article: BBC News article link

Article Summary

BBC News reports on a legal idea called Rights of Nature, which can give a lake, river, or other natural site a form of legal “personhood.” Supporters say this could help Lough Neagh, a major freshwater lake in Northern Ireland that has suffered from pollution and toxic blue-green algal blooms.

The article compares Lough Neagh with Mar Menor, a Spanish saltwater lagoon where campaigners won legal recognition after ecological collapse. The lesson is useful for vocabulary about environmental protection, law, public campaigns, and how societies decide who—or what—can be represented in court.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導「自然權利」這個法律概念：湖泊、河流或其他自然地點，可能被賦予某種法律上的「人格」。支持者認為，這可能有助於北愛爾蘭的 Lough Neagh；這座重要淡水湖近年受到污染與有毒藍綠藻大量繁殖的影響。

文章也比較西班牙的 Mar Menor 鹹水潟湖。當地在生態崩壞後，由倡議者推動並取得法律承認。這篇文章適合學習環境保護、法律、公共倡議，以及社會如何決定誰或什麼可以在法庭上被代表的英文詞彙。

5 Daily Words

legal

connected with the law, courts, or official rules.

In article: “Could recognising Lough Neagh as a legal ‘person’ help restore the largest freshwater lake in these islands?”

法律的、與法律有關的；指和法律、法院或正式規則相關。

recognition

official acceptance that something exists, matters, or has a particular status.

In article: “The former lawyer who secured a Rights of Nature recognition for a Spanish saltwater lagoon believes it could.”

承認、認可；正式接受某事存在、重要，或具有某種地位。

blooms

rapid growths of algae or plants, especially when they spread across water.

In article: “The lough has been blighted by potentially toxic blue-green algal blooms since 2023, due to pollution primarily from agriculture and wastewater.”

大量繁殖、藻華；指藻類或植物快速增生，特別是在水面上擴散的情況。

restoration

the process of bringing something damaged back to a healthier or earlier condition.

In article: “That success has piqued the interest of lawyers and academics in Northern Ireland, who are trying to find ways to support the restoration of Lough Neagh.”

修復、復原；把受損的事物恢復到較健康或較接近原本狀態的過程。

anthropocentric

treating human beings as the central or most important concern.

In article: “We went to the parliament in order to change the existing anthropocentric law into an ecocentric law,” Salazar-Ortuño said.

人類中心的；把人類視為最核心、最重要考量的觀點或制度。

Usage note: This is today’s stretch word. It is common in environmental law and philosophy, but much more formal than human-centred.
