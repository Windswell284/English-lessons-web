US Eases Oil Sanctions as Iran Denies Nuclear Inspectors Claim

Today’s world affairs lesson uses a BBC News report about sanctions relief, nuclear inspections, and early US-Iran talks after conflict.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: US eases oil sanctions as Iran denies Vance claim on nuclear inspectors

Source: BBC News

Published: June 22, 2026

Original article: BBC article link

Article Summary

BBC News reports that the United States has temporarily waived some oil sanctions on Iran after talks in Switzerland, allowing Iranian oil sales in US dollars for the first time in decades. US officials said the move was linked to Iran keeping the Strait of Hormuz open and allowing nuclear inspectors back into the country.

Iran, however, denied making new commitments on inspections. The disagreement shows how fragile early diplomacy can be: both sides may describe the same talks differently while mediators try to build a roadmap toward a final deal.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導，美國在瑞士會談後暫時豁免部分對伊朗石油的制裁，讓伊朗數十年來首次可以用美元出售石油。美方表示，這項安排與伊朗維持霍爾木茲海峽開放、並允許核查人員返回有關。

不過，伊朗否認對核查作出新的承諾。這個分歧顯示，初期外交往往很脆弱：雙方可能對同一次會談有不同說法，而調停方則試圖推動通往最終協議的路線圖。

5 Daily Words

sanctions

official penalties or restrictions used by one country or organization to pressure another country, company, or person.

In article: “It came as the US temporarily waived sanctions, allowing Iran to sell oil in US dollars for the first time in decades.”

制裁；國家或組織為施壓而採取的官方限制或懲罰措施。

Usage note: In news English, sanctions is usually plural: impose sanctions, lift sanctions, waive sanctions.

negotiations

formal discussions between people, governments, or groups that are trying to reach an agreement.

In article: “Following negotiations in Switzerland, Vance said discussions with the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) could be happening ‘as soon as today’.”

談判、協商；為達成協議而進行的正式討論。

Usage note: The verb is negotiate: officials are negotiating a deal.

mediators

neutral people, countries, or organizations that help opposing sides talk and try to reach an agreement.

In article: “In a joint statement released on Monday, mediators Qatar and Pakistan said that after the first round of talks in the Swiss resort of Bürgenstock, the US and Iran had agreed to ‘a roadmap towards reaching a final deal within 60 days’.”

調停者、斡旋方；協助對立雙方溝通並尋求協議的中立人士、國家或組織。

waiver

official permission not to follow a usual rule, restriction, or legal requirement for a limited case or period.

In article: “Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent said that in exchange for the 60-day waiver, Tehran had committed to keeping the vital Strait of Hormuz open and allowing IAEA nuclear inspectors back into the country.”

豁免、免除；在特定情況或期間內，官方允許不適用某項規則、限制或法律要求。

Usage note: A waiver is often temporary and specific, not the same as fully ending a rule.

dismantles

takes apart or removes a system, structure, or policy piece by piece, often in a deliberate and formal way.

In article: “The 60-day sanctions waiver issued by the US Treasury on Monday dismantles central pillars of Washington’s long-running embargo, which has historically choked off Tehran’s economy.”

拆除、逐步廢除；有計畫地把制度、結構或政策一部分一部分移除。

Usage note: This is today’s stretch word. In policy news, dismantle often means to weaken or remove an established system.
