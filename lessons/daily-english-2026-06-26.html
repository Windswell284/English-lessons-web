Special Needs Support Reaches Another Record High

Today’s education and society lesson uses a BBC News report about the growing number of children receiving formal special educational needs support in England.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: Number of children getting special educational needs support hits another record high

Source: BBC News

Published: June 25, 2026

Original article: BBC article link

Article Summary

BBC News reports that the number of children and young people in England with education, health and care plans has risen by 12.5% in a year, reaching more than 700,000. These plans are legal documents that set out the extra support a child should receive when ordinary school provision is not enough.

The government says the figures show the scale of the challenge and points to new investment and reforms aimed at helping more children in mainstream schools. Families, charities, and teaching unions remain concerned about delays, uneven support, and whether future reforms will protect children’s legal rights.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導，英格蘭持有教育、健康與照護計畫（EHCP）的兒童與青少年人數一年內增加 12.5%，總數已超過 70 萬。這些計畫是法律文件，用來列明當一般學校支援不足時，孩子應該獲得哪些額外協助。

英國政府表示，這些數字顯示問題規模龐大，並強調將透過投資與改革，讓更多孩子能在主流學校得到支援。不過，家長、慈善團體與教師工會仍擔心申請延誤、支援不均，以及未來改革是否能保障孩子的法律權利。

5 Daily Words

support

help, services, or resources given to someone so they can manage a situation or succeed.

In article: “Number of children getting special educational needs support hits another record high.”

支援、協助；為了讓某人能應付情況或順利學習、生活而提供的幫助、服務或資源。

transition

the process of changing from one stage, system, or situation to another.

In article: “For mum Karen, getting an EHCP in place before that transition is crucial.”

過渡、轉換；從一個階段、制度或情況轉到另一個的過程。

inclusive

designed to include different kinds of people and make it possible for them to take part fairly.

In article: “Matt Wrack, general secretary of the NASUWT teaching union, criticised the government for issuing guidance on inclusion before publishing the long-awaited response to the SEND consultation… they were being asked to adapt without clear expectations of what an inclusive school must provide.”

具包容性的；設計上能納入不同需求的人，並讓他們能公平參與。

provision

the act of providing something that people need, or the service and resources that are provided.

In article: “She said high-quality provision for SEND pupils was a ‘postcode lottery’, but that the reforms would address that.”

提供、供應；也可指已提供的服務、資源或安排。

Usage note: In education and health policy, provision often means the practical services available to people.

statutory

required, controlled, or created by law.

In article: “The number of plans issued within the statutory timeframe of 20 weeks has decreased slightly, with less than half of plans issued on time.”

法定的；由法律要求、規範或設立的。

Usage note: This is today’s stretch word. It is common in legal, policy, and government reporting.
