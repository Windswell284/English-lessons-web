Free School Meal Pupils Falling Further Behind Their Peers, Report Warns

Today’s Friday society and education lesson looks at widening attainment gaps, early-years support, and how policy choices can affect children from lower-income families.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: Free school meal pupils falling further behind their peers, report warns

Source: BBC News

Published: July 14, 2026

Original article: BBC News article link

Article Summary

BBC News reports on an Education Policy Institute warning that the disadvantage gap in England has widened again since the pandemic. The report says children who have been eligible for free school meals are still behind their better-off classmates at every phase of school, with the difference especially serious in early education.

The article says the government has promised to expand support, including funded childcare and wider free school meal eligibility. Education groups argue that the next prime minister will need a sharp focus on early years, school funding, SEND provision, and assessment reform if the country is to reduce the gap.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導，英國教育政策研究所警告，疫情後英格蘭的教育弱勢差距再次擴大。報告指出，曾符合免費學校餐資格的孩子，在各個學習階段仍落後於較富裕家庭的同學，其中早期教育階段的差距特別明顯。

文章也提到，政府承諾擴大支援，包括增加政府資助的托育服務與擴大免費學校餐資格。教育團體認為，若要縮小差距，下一任首相需要集中處理幼兒教育、學校經費、特殊教育需求支援，以及考試與評量改革等問題。

5 Daily Words

pupils

students, especially children who attend a school.

In article: “Disadvantaged pupils were, on average, 19 months behind their classmates from better-off backgrounds by the time they got to Key Stage 4, the report said.”

學生；特別指在學校就讀的兒童或青少年。

disadvantaged

having fewer social, economic, or educational advantages than other people.

In article: “For its measure of who counts as disadvantaged, the EPI looked at the educational outcomes of children who had ever been eligible for free school meals, compared with those who had not.”

弱勢的；在社會、經濟或教育資源上比其他人處於較不利的位置。

eligible

qualified or allowed to receive something because you meet the rules or requirements.

In article: “The EPI made a number of recommendations in its report for how to achieve it, including widening access to free funded childcare to all children, and increasing the amount of money schools can receive for the number of children they teach who are eligible for free school meals.”

符合資格的；因為達到規定或條件，所以可以獲得某項福利、服務或機會。

attainment

the level of success or achievement someone has reached, especially in education.

In article: “For pupils with SEND, the EPI found that attainment gaps had narrowed for older age groups, but were at their highest levels on record for children who had education, health and care plans.”

成就、達成程度；特別指在教育中已達到的學業表現或水準。

statutory

required, created, or controlled by law; formal and legal in status.

In article: “‘Statutory testing in primary must be ended and assessment approaches in secondary must broaden beyond end-of-course exams to allow all students to achieve and thrive,’ he said.”

法定的；由法律規定、設立或管理的，具有正式法律地位。
