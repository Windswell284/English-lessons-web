Trump Threatens Tariffs Over European Tech Taxes

Today’s Monday business lesson looks at a transatlantic dispute over digital services taxes, trade penalties, and how governments respond when technology policy meets international commerce.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: Trump threatens 100% tariff on European nations over tech tax

Source: BBC News

Published: June 27, 2026

Original article: BBC News article link

Article Summary

BBC News reports that US President Donald Trump threatened a 100% import tariff on European countries that introduce or expand digital services taxes on large American technology companies. He said countries that impose such taxes would face immediate penalties on goods sent to the United States.

The article explains that several European governments already have, or have proposed, taxes on major digital platforms. It also notes that the warning comes soon after a US-EU trade deal, making the story useful for English about tariffs, tax policy, retaliation, trade agreements, and proportional responses.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導，美國總統川普威脅，若歐洲國家對大型美國科技公司推出或擴大數位服務稅，美國將對這些國家課徵 100% 的進口關稅。他表示，凡是徵收這類稅的國家，其輸往美國的商品會立即面臨懲罰性措施。

文章說明，歐洲已有數個政府實施或提出針對大型數位平台的稅制。這項警告又出現在美歐剛完成新貿易協議之後，因此很適合學習關稅、稅務政策、報復措施、貿易協議，以及「按比例回應」等商業與政策英文。

5 Daily Words

tariff

a tax that a government charges on goods imported from another country.

In article: “US president Donald Trump has vowed to impose a 100% import tariff on any European country that introduces a digital services tax on American technology giants.”

關稅；政府對從其他國家進口的商品所課徵的稅。

levy

an official tax, fee, or charge collected by a government or authority.

In article: “Writing on his Truth Social platform, Trump said ‘numerous European countries’ had been discussing bringing in such a levy and some were close to doing so.”

徵費、稅款；由政府或主管機關正式收取的稅、費用或款項。

punitive

intended to punish someone or make the cost of an action very high.

In article: “He warned that the punitive penalties would be applied immediately and would completely ‘supersede’ any existing bilateral trade agreements.”

懲罰性的；目的在於懲罰，或讓某個行動付出很高代價的。

supersede

to replace an older rule, agreement, or system because a newer one takes priority.

In article: “He warned that the punitive penalties would be applied immediately and would completely ‘supersede’ any existing bilateral trade agreements.”

取代、優先於；新的規則、協議或制度取代舊的，並具有更高優先性。

proportionately

in a way that is appropriately balanced in size, strength, or seriousness compared with the situation.

In article: “Michael Damianos, minister of energy, commerce and industry of the Republic of Cyprus, said at the time that ‘the EU can respond swiftly and proportionately when the deal is not respected or its interests are at stake’.”

按比例地、相稱地；回應的規模、力度或嚴重程度與情況相匹配。

Usage note: This is today’s stretch word. In policy and diplomacy, it often describes a response that is firm but not excessive.
