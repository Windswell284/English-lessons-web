A Bug From Pet Stores Helps Scientists Clean Skeletons

Today’s Sunday lighter science lesson looks at how museum researchers may use “superworms” to prepare animal skeletons, with vocabulary for biology, collections, and careful scientific work.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: A bug commonly sold at pet stores is helping scientists with a vexing task

Source: NPR

Published: July 17, 2026

Original article: NPR article link

Article Summary

NPR reports on researchers studying superworms, the larvae of a beetle that is often sold as animal feed, as a practical way to clean animal skeletons for museum and scientific collections. A natural history museum in Mashhad, Iran, received many donated animals and needed a method that would preserve delicate bones without using harsh chemicals or time-consuming boiling.

The researchers found that small groups of superworms could strip flesh from specimens quickly while leaving fragile skeletons mostly intact. Outside experts said the method may be useful for museums with limited facilities, though they cautioned that the worms’ strong jaws could accidentally damage tiny bones and that their feeding behavior may vary.

文章摘要

NPR 報導，研究人員正在研究一種常被當作寵物飼料販售的甲蟲幼蟲「superworms」，看它們是否能用來清理動物骨骼，供博物館與科學收藏使用。伊朗馬什哈德的一座自然史博物館收到許多捐贈動物，需要一種能保護脆弱骨頭、又避免使用強烈化學品或耗時煮沸的方法。

研究發現，少量 superworms 可以快速清除標本上的肉，並大致保留細小骨骼的完整。外部專家認為，這種方法可能有助於設備較有限的博物館；但也提醒，牠們強壯的顎可能不小心折斷小骨頭，而且進食狀況可能不一定穩定。

5 Daily Words

specimens

individual animals, plants, rocks, or objects kept or studied as examples in science.

In article: “Superworms, the larvae of Zophobas morio, are often used as animal feed. But researchers have discovered they also like to feed on animal corpses and can be used to clean specimens.”

標本；在科學研究或收藏中作為樣本保存、觀察的動植物、岩石或物件。

larvae

young insects after they hatch and before they change into their adult form.

In article: “To clean a specimen, the researchers found that 10 to 15 larvae work best.”

幼蟲；昆蟲孵化後、變成成蟲之前的階段。

organism

a living thing, such as an animal, plant, fungus, bacterium, or insect.

In article: “Researchers use a range of techniques to strip the flesh off an organism, but each has its drawbacks.”

生物、有機體；動物、植物、真菌、細菌或昆蟲等活的生命體。

mandibles

the jaw-like mouthparts of an insect or similar animal, used for biting or cutting food.

In article: “Each one is nearly the size of a human finger, they have large chewing mandibles, and they're available as feed at many pet stores.”

大顎、顎器；昆蟲或類似動物用來咬碎或切割食物的顎狀口器。

inadvertently

without intending to do something; accidentally, especially when a small action causes an unwanted result.

In article: “He says that superworms may well be easier to control than dermestid beetles, but he worries that the superworms' powerful mandibles could inadvertently snap a small bone.”

無意中、不慎地；不是故意做某事，卻可能意外造成不想要的結果。
