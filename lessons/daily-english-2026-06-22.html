Backstage at Gorillaz’ Epic One-Off Stadium Show

Today’s lighter lesson uses a BBC music feature about Gorillaz, backstage energy, and a huge one-night stadium show.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: Backstage at Gorillaz’ epic, one-off stadium show: ‘The vibe is ridiculous’

Source: BBC News

Published: June 21, 2026

Original article: BBC article link

Article Summary

BBC News goes backstage at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium as Gorillaz prepare for their first ever stadium show. The piece describes a busy, cheerful scene with Damon Albarn, guest musicians, rehearsals, costumes, food, and a surprisingly relaxed atmosphere before a huge performance.

Instead of a heavy political story, this article is about music, collaboration, and the playful chaos behind a big live event. It gives us useful English for describing concerts, creative teams, and the feeling of being behind the scenes.

文章摘要

BBC News 帶讀者到 Tottenham Hotspur Stadium 的後台，看 Gorillaz 準備他們第一次的體育場演出。文章描寫一個忙碌但開心的場景：Damon Albarn、客座音樂人、彩排、服裝、食物，以及大型表演前意外輕鬆的氣氛。

這篇文章不是沉重的政治新聞，而是關於音樂、合作，以及大型現場活動背後有點混亂又好玩的準備過程。它提供了很多實用英文，可以用來描述演唱會、創作團隊和幕後氛圍。

5 Daily Words

backstage

in or around the private area behind a stage, where performers prepare before a show.

In article: “Damon Albarn, De La Soul and Moonchild Sannelly talk backstage as Gorillaz play their biggest show.”

後台的、在後台；表演者上台前準備的舞台後方區域。

Usage note: You can use backstage literally at a concert, or more generally for “behind-the-scenes” access.

rehearsals

practice sessions before a performance, speech, ceremony, or other planned event.

In article: “It’s Friday night at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium, and he’s in the middle of rehearsals for Gorillaz’ first ever stadium show…”

彩排、排練；正式演出或活動前的練習。

Usage note: The singular form is rehearsal: a band rehearsal, a wedding rehearsal, a dress rehearsal.

refrain

a repeated line or group of lines in a song or poem.

In article: “...a cartoon choir, singing the song’s refrain, ‘all I do is dance’.”

副歌、反覆句；歌曲或詩中重複出現的一句或一段。

Usage note: In music writing, refrain is close to “chorus,” but it can sound a little more literary.

entourage

a group of people who travel with or support an important person, performer, or team.

In article: “Albarn’s laid-back vibes set the tone for the whole entourage.”

隨行團隊、陪同人員；跟著重要人物、表演者或團隊一起行動的人。

Usage note: Entourage is common in entertainment, sports, and politics.

scintilla

a very tiny amount of something, usually used in a phrase like “not a scintilla of doubt.”

In article: “Backstage at Tottenham, there are more than 30 musicians from 15 different countries, and not a scintilla of ego between them.”

一丁點、極少量；常用在 “not a scintilla of...” 表示完全沒有某種東西。

Usage note: This is the stretch word today. It is formal and memorable, but you will usually see it in writing rather than casual speech.
