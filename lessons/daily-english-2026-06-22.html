Burnham Ally to Unveil Ambitious Plan to Reverse Decades of Privatisation

Today’s business and economy lesson uses a Guardian report on a policy paper calling for more state control over essential services.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: Burnham ally to unveil ambitious plan to reverse decades of privatisation

Source: The Guardian

Published: June 21, 2026

Original article: The Guardian article link

Article Summary

The Guardian reports that an ally of Greater Manchester mayor Andy Burnham is preparing a policy paper that argues for reversing decades of privatisation. The proposal says government should regain control of basic services as part of a broader economic agenda connected to “Manchesterism.”

The article frames the plan as an ambitious attempt to make everyday essentials more affordable. It also places the proposal within a larger political debate over whether public ownership can improve services, lower costs, and respond to public frustration after years of private-sector delivery.

文章摘要

《衛報》報導，曼徹斯特市長 Andy Burnham 的一名盟友即將提出一份政策文件，主張扭轉數十年來的私有化趨勢。該方案認為，政府應重新掌握基本服務的控制權，作為與「Manchesterism」相關的更廣泛經濟路線之一。

文章把這項計畫描述為一個雄心勃勃的嘗試，希望讓日常基本需求更負擔得起。它也反映了更大的政治辯論：公共所有權是否能改善服務、降低成本，並回應民眾對多年私人部門提供服務的不滿。

5 Daily Words

affordable

not too expensive; priced so that ordinary people can pay for it.

In article: “Productive State policy paper envisages state regaining control of basics to make life affordable, in fleshing out of Manchesterism.”

負擔得起的；價格不會太高，一般人能夠支付。

Usage note: In policy news, affordable often describes housing, energy, childcare, food, or transport.

reverse

to change something back to an earlier state or move it in the opposite direction.

In article: “Andy Burnham’s government should reverse 40 years of privatisation with a long-term plan to take over the control of basic assets…”

扭轉、逆轉；把某件事改回以前的狀態，或讓它朝相反方向發展。

Usage note: Reverse is common in policy reporting: reverse cuts, reverse a decision, reverse a trend.

regaining

getting back something that was lost, reduced, or taken away.

In article: “Productive State policy paper envisages state regaining control of basics to make life affordable…”

重新取得、恢復；把失去、減少或被拿走的東西重新拿回來。

Usage note: The base verb is regain; it is often used with control, confidence, strength, or territory.

privatisation

the process of moving a service, industry, or asset from government ownership to private ownership.

In article: “Exclusive: Productive State policy paper envisages state regaining control of basics to make life affordable, in fleshing out of Manchesterism. Andy Burnham’s government should reverse 40 years of privatisation…”

私有化；把服務、產業或資產從政府所有轉為私人所有的過程。

Usage note: British English spells it privatisation; American English usually spells it privatization.

envisages

formally imagines or plans something as a likely future possibility.

In article: “Productive State policy paper envisages state regaining control of basics to make life affordable, in fleshing out of Manchesterism.”

設想、構想；正式地把某件事想像或規劃為可能的未來情況。

Usage note: Envisages is a formal news word often used for proposals, strategies, and policy plans.
