Why an AI Company Cleaned My New York City Apartment for Free

Today’s lesson uses a BBC feature on AI companies offering free home services so workers can record household tasks and train future robots.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: Why an AI company cleaned my New York City apartment for free

Source: BBC News

Published: June 20, 2026

Original article: BBC News article link

Article Summary

BBC News reports on an AI company sending free cleaners into New York City apartments while recording their work with cameras. The company says the videos will help train robots to handle ordinary household tasks, because real homes contain different objects, lighting, tools, and messy conditions.

The article also raises privacy and labor concerns. Digital-rights experts warn that people may underestimate how sensitive in-home recordings can be, while privacy advocates question whether a free service is fair compensation for valuable household data that could later be sold to robotics and AI firms.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導，一家 AI 公司在紐約市提供免費居家清潔服務，但清潔人員會戴著攝影設備記錄工作過程。該公司表示，這些影片可用來訓練未來的機器人，因為真實家庭中的物品、光線、工具與環境都各不相同。

文章同時指出隱私與勞動市場的疑慮。數位權利專家警告，人們可能低估居家錄影會收集到多敏感的資訊；隱私倡議者也質疑，免費服務是否足以交換之後可能被出售給機器人與 AI 公司的珍貴家庭資料。

5 Daily Words

initiative

a new plan, project, or organized action designed to solve a problem or create an opportunity.

In article: “The initiative, dubbed Shift by AI firm Micro AGI, is part of a growing number of companies developing the next generation of autonomous robots…”

倡議、計畫；為了解決問題或創造機會而推出的新方案或有組織的行動。

Usage note: Initiative is common in business, government, and technology news.

autonomous

able to operate or make decisions independently, without direct human control.

In article: “…part of a growing number of companies developing the next generation of autonomous robots, which tech bosses hope will be able to do everything from the washing up to serving as live-in personal carers.”

自主的、自動運作的；能在沒有直接人類控制下運作或做決定。

Usage note: This word often appears with robots, vehicles, weapons, and software systems.

dexterity

skill and control in using the hands or body, especially for careful physical tasks.

In article: “The main aim of the offer is to perform tasks requiring dexterity, to train the robots of the future to use their hands.”

靈巧、手部靈活度；尤其指做精細動作時，手或身體的控制能力。

Usage note: Dexterity can also describe mental quickness, but here it means physical hand skill.

anonymised

changed so that the people connected to data cannot be directly identified.

In article: “The company’s business model relies on it being able to sell the valuable data it gathers from inside people’s homes, anonymised, to robotics and other AI companies to train robots.”

匿名化的；資料經過處理，使相關個人無法被直接辨識。

Usage note: British English often uses anonymised; American English usually spells it anonymized.

diabolically

in an extremely clever but harmful, cruel, or morally troubling way.

In article: “And Calli Schroeder, director of the AI and human rights programme at the Electronic Privacy Information Center (Epic), said Shift’s move was ‘a diabolically creative way to sell privacy invasion’.”

惡魔般地、極其陰險地；形容某事非常聰明卻有害、殘酷或道德上令人不安。

Usage note: This is a strong, expressive adverb; in news, it usually appears inside a quoted opinion rather than as neutral reporting.
