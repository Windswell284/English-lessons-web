Iran Oil Tankers Cross Blockade Line

Today’s lesson uses a BBC News report on Iranian oil tankers passing a US naval blockade line in the Gulf of Oman, with vocabulary for maritime trade, sanctions, and enforcement.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: “Strait of Hormuz: Iran sends loaded oil tankers past US naval blockade”

Source: BBC News

Published: June 17, 2026

Original article: BBC News article link

Article Summary

BBC News reported that three Iranian tankers carrying crude oil crossed a US blockade line in the Gulf of Oman, according to ship-tracking data. The movement came after President Donald Trump announced the immediate removal of the blockade, while US naval forces said it would remain in effect until a deal with Iran was signed.

The article says the tankers are owned by the National Iranian Tanker Company, which has been sanctioned by the US Treasury. Analysts told BBC Verify that the crossings suggest Iran is confident the blockade is ending, while maritime data also shows other Iranian-linked vessels becoming more active globally.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導，船舶追蹤資料顯示，三艘載有原油的伊朗油輪已穿越美國在阿曼灣的封鎖線。這一行動發生在川普總統宣布「立即解除」封鎖之後，但美國海軍仍表示，在與伊朗的協議簽署前，封鎖會繼續有效。

報導指出，這些油輪屬於被美國財政部制裁的伊朗國家油輪公司。分析人士告訴 BBC Verify，這些船隻穿越封鎖線顯示伊朗相信封鎖即將結束；同時，海事資料也顯示，其他與伊朗相關的船隻在全球範圍內變得更加活躍。

5 Daily Words

blockade

an official action that uses ships, troops, or other controls to stop goods or people from entering or leaving a place.

In article: “Three Iranian tankers loaded with crude oil have passed the US blockade line in the Gulf of Oman, ship-tracking data shows.”

封鎖；以船艦、軍隊或其他管制手段阻止貨物或人員進出某地的正式行動。

Usage note: Blockade is stronger and more formal than a simple ban because it suggests physical enforcement.

sanctioned

officially punished or restricted by a government or international body, often through trade or financial limits.

In article: “The three Iran-flagged tankers, Diona, Hero II and Sonia I, are all owned by the National Iranian Tanker Company which has been sanctioned by the US Treasury, as have the ships themselves.”

受到制裁的；被政府或國際機構正式懲罰或限制，通常涉及貿易或金融限制。

Usage note: In politics and finance, sanctioned usually means punished, although in other contexts it can mean officially approved.

designated

officially named, classified, or assigned a particular status by an authority.

In article: “Iran has been subject to long-term US sanctions in response to fears the country is developing nuclear weapons, its support for groups designated by Washington as terrorist organisations, and alleged human rights abuses.”

被指定的、被認定的；由主管機關正式命名、分類或賦予某種身分。

Usage note: Designated is common in legal, diplomatic, and security writing.

intercepting

stopping, seizing, or interrupting something while it is moving from one place to another.

In article: “The US had said early in its blockade that enforcement could happen outside of the Gulf region and BBC Verify has previously covered American forces intercepting Iran-linked vessels in the Indian Ocean, thousands of miles from the Gulf.”

攔截、截停；在某物從一處移動到另一處時加以阻止、扣押或中斷。

Usage note: Military and police reports often use intercept for ships, aircraft, messages, or shipments.

unladen

not carrying cargo or a load; empty for transport purposes.

In article: “The unladen tanker has been circling off the Pakistani port of Karachi since 8 May, ship tracking data shows.”

未載貨的、空載的；在運輸意義上沒有裝載貨物或負載。

Usage note: This is a harder formal shipping word. It is most common in transport, maritime, and insurance contexts.
