Israel and Hezbollah Agree Ceasefire, US Says

Today’s lesson uses a BBC report on a US-announced ceasefire between Israel and Hezbollah, and the diplomatic pressure created by continued violence in Lebanon.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: Israel and Hezbollah agree ceasefire, US says, as more Lebanon strikes reported

Source: BBC News

Published: June 19, 2026

Original article: BBC News article link

Article Summary

BBC News reports that Israel and Hezbollah have agreed to a ceasefire, according to a US official, after intense Israeli air strikes in southern Lebanon killed dozens of people. The agreement comes at a sensitive moment because clashes in Lebanon could weaken a broader effort to end the war between the US and Iran.

The report says both sides face pressure from hardliners and from events on the ground. Israel says it will keep responding to immediate threats, while Hezbollah has not fully confirmed the ceasefire. Lebanese officials reported additional strikes after the ceasefire began, leaving many displaced people doubtful that the agreement will hold.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導，美國官員表示以色列與真主黨已同意停火；此前，以色列在黎巴嫩南部發動密集空襲，造成多人死亡。這項停火發生在敏感時刻，因為黎巴嫩的持續衝突可能削弱美國與伊朗之間結束戰爭的更大協議。

報導指出，雙方都受到強硬派與現場局勢的壓力。以色列表示仍會處理「立即威脅」，真主黨則尚未完全確認停火。黎巴嫩官員稱停火開始後仍有空襲，因此許多流離失所的民眾懷疑協議能否真正維持。

5 Daily Words

ceasefire

an agreement to stop fighting for a period of time, often while talks continue.

In article: “Israel and Hezbollah have agreed a ceasefire, a US official says, following intense Israeli air strikes in southern Lebanon that killed 47 people.”

停火；交戰雙方同意在一段時間內停止戰鬥，通常是為了談判或降低衝突。

Usage note: Ceasefire is common in news about wars, diplomacy, and peace talks.

clashes

short fights, violent incidents, or serious disagreements between opposing groups.

In article: “The latest agreement followed concerns that continued clashes, which also saw Hezbollah kill four Israeli soldiers in Lebanon, would undermine the deal to end the war between the US and Iran.”

衝突、交火；敵對群體之間短暫但激烈的打鬥、暴力事件或嚴重對立。

Usage note: News reports often use clashes when fighting is limited but still dangerous.

escalation

a situation in which a conflict, problem, or action becomes more serious or intense.

In article: “The deadly escalation is another sign that Donald Trump is not necessarily in control of the fate of his deal with Iran.”

升級、惡化；衝突、問題或行動變得更嚴重、更激烈的情況。

Usage note: Escalation can describe military action, political disputes, costs, or workplace conflicts.

memorandum

a formal written note or document that records an agreement, decision, or official understanding.

In article: “The memorandum of understanding declared a ceasefire in Lebanon as well as between the US and Iran.”

備忘錄、正式書面文件；用來記錄協議、決定或官方共識的文件。

Usage note: In diplomacy and business, a memorandum of understanding records shared intentions, though it may not be a full treaty or contract.

unprecedented

never having happened, existed, or been done before; without a known earlier example.

In article: “Trump himself has given fuel to this argument in an unprecedented set of accusations against his ally, Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu…”

前所未有的、史無前例的；以前沒有發生過、存在過或被做過的。

Usage note: This is a strong formal word; use it only when something is genuinely unusual or without precedent.
