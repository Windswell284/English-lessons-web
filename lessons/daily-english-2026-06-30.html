South African Leader Warns Anti-Migrant Protesters

Today’s Tuesday world-affairs lesson looks at migration, protest rights, public safety, and the language leaders use when social tension risks turning into violence.

Level: intermediateTime: 5–10 minutes
Source article

Title: South African leader warns anti-migrant protesters ahead of unofficial deadline

Source: BBC News

Published: June 29, 2026

Original article: BBC News article link

Article Summary

BBC News reports that South African President Cyril Ramaphosa warned people planning anti-migrant protests not to use intimidation, threats, or ultimatums. The warning came before an unofficial deadline set by activists for undocumented foreigners to leave the country.

The article says several thousand migrants from other African countries have already left South Africa in recent weeks because they fear violence. Ramaphosa said protest and free expression are protected rights, but those rights do not allow people to threaten others, damage property, or use violence.

文章摘要

BBC News 報導，南非總統拉瑪佛沙警告，計畫參與反移民抗議的人不應使用恐嚇、威脅或最後通牒。這項警告出現在一個非官方期限前夕；反移民人士要求沒有合法文件的外國人離開南非。

文章指出，近幾週已有數千名來自其他非洲國家的移民因擔心暴力而離開南非。拉瑪佛沙表示，抗議與言論自由受到保障，但這些權利並不允許人們威脅他人、破壞財物或使用暴力。

5 Daily Words

migrants

people who move from one place or country to another, often to work, study, join family, or seek safety.

In article: “South African leader warns anti-migrant protesters ahead of unofficial deadline.”

移民、遷徙者；因工作、就學、家庭或安全等原因從一地或一國移往另一地的人。

undocumented

not having the official papers, records, or legal status required by a government system.

In article: “People intending to protest against migrants should do so without ‘intimidation, threats or ultimatums’, President Cyril Ramaphosa has warned on the eve of an unofficial deadline for undocumented foreigners to leave South Africa.”

無正式文件的、未登記的；沒有政府制度要求的正式證件、紀錄或合法身分。

intimidation

the act of frightening or pressuring people so they feel forced to do something or stay silent.

In article: “People intending to protest against migrants should do so without ‘intimidation, threats or ultimatums’, President Cyril Ramaphosa has warned on the eve of an unofficial deadline for undocumented foreigners to leave South Africa.”

恐嚇、威嚇；讓他人害怕或感到壓力，迫使其行動或保持沉默的做法。

reforms

changes made to laws, systems, or institutions in order to improve how they work.

In article: “As protests have ramped up in recent months, Ramaphosa has repeatedly warned demonstrators to act peacefully and responsibly, while also accepting the need for immigration reforms.”

改革、改良措施；為改善法律、制度或機構運作而做出的改變。

ultimatums

final demands that leave the other side with a choice between obeying or facing serious consequences.

In article: “People intending to protest against migrants should do so without ‘intimidation, threats or ultimatums’, President Cyril Ramaphosa has warned on the eve of an unofficial deadline for undocumented foreigners to leave South Africa.”

最後通牒；要求對方照做，否則將面臨嚴重後果的最終要求。

Usage note: This is today’s stretch word. It is common in diplomacy, labour disputes, and crisis reporting when one side sets a hard condition.
