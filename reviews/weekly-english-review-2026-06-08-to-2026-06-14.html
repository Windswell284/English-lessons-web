Key Words & Phrases to Keep

Diplomacy & agreements
negotiate · obligations · envisages

Use these when describing what an agreement tries to do and what each side must do.

  • negotiate = discuss terms to reach an agreement.
  • obligations = duties required by an agreement or law.
  • envisages = formally plans or imagines a future result.
Cautious news wording
does not rule out · casts doubt · appeared stuck

These phrases let journalists report uncertainty without sounding emotional.

  • does not rule out = says something remains possible.
  • casts doubt = makes a claim or schedule seem uncertain.
  • appeared stuck = seemed unable to make progress.
Risk & pressure
threatens · raising fears · chokehold

Good for climate, security, and economic stories where danger or pressure is increasing.

  • threatens = may cause harm or create risk.
  • raising fears = causing worry about a possible problem.
  • chokehold = strong control over a route, market, or situation.
Step-by-step change
staged reintegration · incrementally · under way

Use these to describe a process that has begun but will not finish all at once.

  • under way = already started and in progress.
  • incrementally = gradually, in small steps.
  • staged reintegration = a planned step-by-step return to a system.

Useful Patterns

Pattern 1

Subject + does not rule out + noun/-ing

The company does not rule out price rises.

Pattern 2

Event + threatens + consequence

El Niño threatens weather extremes and higher temperatures.

Pattern 3

Agreement + envisages + outcome

The agreement envisages a staged reintegration into the global economy.

Pattern 4

Place/capital city + verb

Tehran casts doubt on the timing. Here, Tehran means Iran’s government.

Common Mistakes & Stronger Alternatives

Weak: The talks did not move.

Stronger: The talks appeared stuck.

Weak: The company may raise prices.

Stronger: The company does not rule out price rises.

Weak: The weather may be bad.

Stronger: The pattern threatens weather extremes.

Weak: The plan will happen slowly.

Stronger: The measures will happen incrementally.

Mistake: underway is always one word.

Better: In formal British-style news, under way as two words is common.

Mistake: staged means fake.

Better: In staged reintegration, staged means done in stages.
